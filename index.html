Finding Pictures and Graphics for Inspiration

Developments in computer technology and networks have made the Internet a powerful, universal resource for almost everything. You can learn how to bake a cake, replace a car window, and even how to formulate hair products. The Internet has especially become an invaluable resource for images and graphics. With businesses and individuals continually creating and changing websites, blogs, and ecommerce stores, images and graphics have become a vital necessity in the online world.

The high demand for images and graphics has sparked the emergence of websites that provide various levels of access to images for personal, commercial, and editorial use. Some sites offer images for free with no restrictions, while many charge subscription fees or require licensed permission.Fortunately, whether you plan to use free or purchased images, you can view all kinds of images online for brainstorming purposes and for inspiration. Viewing non-free graphics and images, for example, can spark ideas for creative concepts and project themes that can be used to find free images.

Here are a few websites that offer high-quality images and require paid subscriptions.

  • Clipart Of
  • Bigstock
  • Shutterstock
  • Stock.xchng

There are also sites that provide online photo management and sharing. These sites allow users to upload photos and share them with networks of people. Site visitors can also view these pictures. The policies for using photos for personal use vary from site to site, but many of them require submitting requests to the image owners for licensing or special permission. Here are a few examples of such sites.

  • Flickr
  • Picasa
  • Pinterest

Type in a search term and click Search or press Enter to view images from Bigstock and Flickr.
